The Fundamental Shift

This wave is different from the last one

The GCCs of the 2010s were cost plays. The GCCs arriving in India from 2024 onward are strategic bets. The companies making those bets need a different kind of partner — one who understands both the intelligence architecture and the ground truth of India.

Wave 1 — 2010 to 2020

The Cost Centre Model

Companies came to India for labour arbitrage. Functions were defined at HQ, handed down to India, and measured on cost per ticket.

  • IT support and helpdesk offshoring
  • Finance and accounting process lift-and-shift
  • HR shared services and payroll
  • Dependent on HQ for every strategic decision
  • Seen as a cost line, not a value centre
Wave 2 — 2024 Onward

The Intelligence Centre Model

Companies are coming to India for strategic depth. The GCC is no longer a back-office. It is the enterprise's thinking engine in Asia.

  • AI and data science as anchor functions
  • Product engineering and R&D — not just delivery
  • Global ownership of entire function P&Ls
  • Innovation Labs and IP creation on Indian soil
  • GCC as a recruiting brand, not a cost allocation
